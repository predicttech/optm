# Sciatica

Sciatica is a condition that occurs when the sciatic nerve—the longest and largest nerve in the human body—becomes irritated, inflamed, or compressed. Rather than being a disease itself, sciatica is a symptom of an underlying problem affecting the lower back or pelvis.

The pain usually starts in the lower back or buttock and travels down one leg, sometimes reaching the foot. Depending on the severity, sciatica can range from mild discomfort to intense, burning pain that makes walking, sitting, or standing difficult.

Most cases improve with appropriate treatment, but persistent or severe symptoms require medical evaluation to prevent long-term nerve damage.

---

# What is Sciatica?

The sciatic nerve originates in the lower spine and travels through the hips, buttocks, and down each leg.

When this nerve is compressed or irritated, it can cause pain, numbness, tingling, or weakness anywhere along its path.

Sciatica usually affects only one side of the body, although both sides can occasionally be involved.

---

# How Common is Sciatica?

Sciatica is one of the most common causes of leg pain.

It frequently affects:

- Adults between 30 and 60 years of age
- Office workers
- Drivers
- Manual laborers
- People with physically demanding jobs
- Individuals with obesity
- People with chronic lower back pain

---

# What Causes Sciatica?

Several conditions can compress or irritate the sciatic nerve.

## 1. Herniated Disc

A slipped or herniated disc is the most common cause of sciatica. The damaged disc presses on nearby nerve roots.

---

## 2. Degenerative Disc Disease

As spinal discs wear down with age, they may irritate nearby nerves.

---

## 3. Spinal Stenosis

Narrowing of the spinal canal can compress the sciatic nerve.

---

## 4. Muscle Tightness

Tight muscles in the hips or buttocks, particularly the piriformis muscle, may compress the sciatic nerve.

---

## 5. Muscle Weakness

Weak core, hip, and back muscles reduce spinal stability and increase pressure on the lower back.

---

## 6. Injury

Falls, accidents, or sports injuries can damage structures around the sciatic nerve.

---

## 7. Pregnancy

Hormonal changes and increased pressure on the lower spine may temporarily cause sciatica during pregnancy.

---

# Symptoms of Sciatica

Symptoms usually occur on one side of the body.

Common symptoms include:

- Lower back pain
- Buttock pain
- Pain radiating down the leg
- Burning sensation
- Sharp or shooting pain
- Tingling in the leg or foot
- Numbness
- Muscle weakness
- Pain while sitting
- Difficulty standing for long periods
- Pain that worsens when coughing or sneezing

---

# Who is at Risk?

Your risk increases if you:

- Are between 30 and 60 years old
- Sit for prolonged periods
- Lift heavy objects frequently
- Have poor posture
- Are overweight
- Have diabetes
- Have weak core muscles
- Smoke
- Previously injured your back

---

# At What Age Does Sciatica Occur?

### Young Adults (20–35)

- Disc injuries
- Sports injuries
- Heavy lifting

### Middle Age (35–55)

- Herniated discs
- Poor posture
- Sedentary lifestyle

### Older Adults (55+)

- Spinal stenosis
- Degenerative disc disease
- Arthritis

---

# When Should You See a Doctor?

Seek medical attention if:

- Pain lasts longer than two weeks
- Pain is severe
- Walking becomes difficult
- You develop numbness or weakness
- You lose bladder or bowel control (medical emergency)
- Symptoms worsen despite rest
- Pain follows a serious injury

---

# How is Sciatica Diagnosed?

Diagnosis usually includes:

## Medical History

Your healthcare provider will ask about:

- Pain location
- Symptom duration
- Previous injuries
- Daily activities

---

## Physical Examination

Assessment may include:

- Walking pattern
- Muscle strength
- Reflexes
- Straight leg raise test
- Sensation testing

---

## Imaging Tests

Depending on symptoms, imaging may include:

- MRI
- X-ray
- CT Scan

---

# Treatment for Sciatica

Treatment depends on the underlying cause.

## Physical Therapy

Physical therapy can help:

- Strengthen core muscles
- Improve posture
- Increase flexibility
- Reduce nerve compression
- Improve movement

---

## Exercise

Recommended exercises may include:

- Core strengthening
- Hamstring stretches
- Hip mobility exercises
- Walking
- Gentle spinal mobility exercises

---

## Lifestyle Changes

Helpful lifestyle modifications include:

- Maintaining a healthy weight
- Avoiding prolonged sitting
- Improving posture
- Using proper lifting techniques
- Staying physically active

---

## Medications

Doctors may recommend:

- Pain relievers
- Anti-inflammatory medications
- Muscle relaxants

Medication should always be taken under professional guidance.

---

## Injections

Some patients may benefit from epidural steroid injections to reduce nerve inflammation.

---

## Surgery

Surgery may be recommended if:

- Conservative treatment fails
- Severe nerve compression exists
- Progressive muscle weakness develops
- Bladder or bowel function is affected

---

# Can Sciatica Be Prevented?

Many cases are preventable.

### Prevention Tips

- Exercise regularly
- Strengthen your core muscles
- Maintain good posture
- Lift heavy objects correctly
- Avoid prolonged sitting
- Stretch regularly
- Maintain a healthy weight
- Stay physically active

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Does sciatica always start in the back?

No. Some people experience pain mainly in the buttock or leg without significant lower back pain.

---

## Is walking good for sciatica?

In many cases, gentle walking helps reduce stiffness and promotes recovery, but severe pain should be assessed before beginning exercise.

---

## Can sciatica heal without surgery?

Yes. Most people recover with conservative treatments such as physical therapy, exercise, and lifestyle changes.

---

## How long does sciatica last?

Mild cases may improve within a few weeks, while chronic or severe cases may take several months depending on the underlying cause.

---

## Can sitting too long make sciatica worse?

Yes. Prolonged sitting increases pressure on the lower spine and can aggravate sciatic nerve irritation.
