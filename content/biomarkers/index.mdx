# Biomarkers

Biomarkers are measurable biological indicators that provide valuable insights into your body's health. They help identify changes occurring at the molecular, cellular, and tissue levels—often before symptoms become severe.

At OPTM, biomarkers play a central role in understanding the biological drivers of joint pain, muscle weakness, reduced mobility, and musculoskeletal aging. Rather than relying solely on symptoms or imaging, biomarker testing helps uncover the underlying processes affecting your muscles, bones, cartilage, and overall movement health.

By evaluating multiple biomarkers together, clinicians can better understand inflammation, tissue degeneration, bone remodeling, muscle function, oxidative stress, and the body's ability to heal.

# Why Biomarkers Matter

Biomarkers provide objective information about what is happening inside your body. They can help identify inflammation, tissue damage, nutritional deficiencies, impaired healing, or abnormal bone and muscle metabolism—even before significant structural damage occurs.

When interpreted alongside your symptoms, medical history, and physical examination, biomarkers can support earlier diagnosis, more personalized treatment plans, and better monitoring of recovery over time.

# Biomarkers and Musculoskeletal Health

No single biomarker tells the complete story. Instead, multiple biomarkers work together to provide a more comprehensive understanding of your biological health.

Different biomarkers may reflect:

- Inflammation
- Bone formation
- Cartilage breakdown
- Muscle injury
- Tissue repair
- Oxidative stress
- Nerve health
- Healthy aging

By combining these biological insights with clinical evaluation, healthcare professionals can better identify the factors contributing to pain, weakness, stiffness, and reduced mobility.
